How to play:

Objective
Get a hand total as close to 21 as possible without going over, and have a higher total than the dealer.

Card Values
Number cards (2–10) are worth their face value.
Jack, Queen, and King are worth 10.
Ace is worth 1 or 11, whichever benefits the hand more.

Setup
The computer is the dealer.

The Deal
Each player receives two cards face up.
The dealer receives two cards: one face up and one face down.

Player’s Turn
Starting to the dealer’s left, each player decides how to play their hand. The options are:
Hit: Take another card.
Stand: Keep the current hand and end the turn.
Split: If the first two cards are the same value, split them into two separate hands.
The player may continue to hit until they choose to stand or their total exceeds 21. If the total exceeds 21, the player busts and loses.

Dealer's (Computer's) Turn
After all players finish, the dealer will reveal the face-down card.
The computer will hit if the total is 16 or less.
The computer will stand if the total is 17 or more.
If the computer busts, all remaining players win.

Winning
If a player’s total is higher than the computer’s without going over 21, the player wins.
If the computer’s total is higher without going over 21, the player loses.
If both totals are the same, it is a push.

Blackjack
A blackjack is an Ace and a 10-value card dealt as the first two cards. It automatically wins against any other hand except another blackjack.
